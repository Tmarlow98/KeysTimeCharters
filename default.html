About Captain Tyler Marlow

I graduated from Florida Gulf Coast Univeristy in May 2020 with a Bachelor's Degree of Science in Software Engineering. I grew up in Plantation, Florida and went to high school at St. Thomas Aquinas. I graduated during the COVID-19 outbreak. Due to this pandemic, I found myself with some free time immediately following graduation so I brought it upon myself to start my charter fishing business which has been one of my personal goals. The website you are looking at now has been coded from scratch. I chose to do this because I love challenging myself and learning new things.
Besides my love for coding, I have an extreme passion for fishing. I was first introduced to this amazing sport when I was 4 years old. When I caught my first bass I knew I was hooked for life. As I grew older I became more interested in saltwater fishing. When I was in 8th grade I went backcountry fishing in The Everglades for the first time and fell in love. Give me a call and let's catch some fish!
Some of the fish we will be targeting are Redfish, Snook and Tarpon. These three species are the most popular game fish in Everglades National Park. There are also other amazing species too, including Triple Tail, Sea Trout, Jack Creval, Mangrove Snapper and Lady Fish. There is also a good chance you will see a pod of dolphins, sea turtles, bald eagles, and other wildlife native to Florida. These are common sightings while in the backcountry.

The Gear

Back to Top
I have been running the 2003 Dolphin Super Skiff Kevlar for almost 8 years now. This skiff gives us the perfect balance of shallow water accessibility along with the ability to maintain a dry relaxing ride through the thickest of chop. The skiff is powered by a 2006 evinrude e-tec 2 stroke outboard motor. The skiff has 2 live wells along with 6 rod holders that will keep them in a safe posotion. There is also a large storage compartment in the back that will keep all your belongings dry and secure throughout the day.
Wondering how to keep your lunch nice and cool? The yeti cooler is the most hardy cooler on the market. One bag of ice is all you need for an entire day of fishing in the hot florida sun. I have been using yeti coolers since I began backcountry fishing. There is nothing better than taking a lunch break after catching fish all day knowing your perishables will be as if you took them out of your own refridgerator.
The Gloomis E6x Inshore 7'6 rod is my favorite light tackle fishing rod. There are many reasons why I choose this rod. The main reason for chosing this rod is because of the distance you get out of ever cast. An effortless swing and your bait soars through the sky landing in the honey hole. Long casting is essential because fish can be spooky, so keeping your distance and having a rod that can reach the fish from a distance can make all the differance.
